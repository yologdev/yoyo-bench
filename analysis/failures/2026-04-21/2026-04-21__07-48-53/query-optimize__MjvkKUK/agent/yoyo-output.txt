## Optimizations Applied

**Original query problems:**
1. Repeated correlated subquery — top_synsetid used a correlated subquery scanning senses for every output row. Then top_synset_sense_count ran *another correlated subquery that contained yet another* nested correlated subquery (to re-compute top_synsetid again). This means 3 full `senses` scans per output row.

Optimized approach:

1. `sense_counts` CTE — Aggregates `(wordid, synsetid, count)` in a single pass, computed once and reused. Filtered early with WHERE wordid IN (SELECT wordid FROM agg) to only process words that pass the HAVING conditions.

2. **ranked CTE with `ROW_NUMBER() window function** — Assigns ranks per wordid ordered by cnt DESC, synsetid ASC` in a single pass, completely eliminating the correlated/nested subqueries. Picking rn = 1 gives the top synset cleanly.

3. **Single scan, no correlation** — The final join between agg and ranked on wordid is a simple equi-join, replacing what was previously O(n) nested subquery executions.
