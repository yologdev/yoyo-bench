## Analysis of the original query's bottleneck

The original query had two major performance problems:
1. Correlated subqueries in the SELECT list — for every qualifying row, top_synsetid executed a full scan of `senses grouped by synset, and top_synset_sense_count` ran two more nested scans of senses to get the same synset again. With potentially thousands of qualifying words × 3 scans each, this was extremely slow.
2. **No indexes** on senses(wordid) or senses(synsetid) (and the DB is read-only, so we can't add them).

## Optimization strategy

The optimized query uses **CTEs to materialize intermediate results** and compute the top synset only once per word:

1. `base_agg` CTE — joins senses × synsets once to compute per-word aggregates with the HAVING filters. SQLite materializes this.
2. `synset_counts` CTE — scans senses a second time (only for qualifying wordids from base_agg) to get per-`(wordid, synsetid) sense counts. This is reused for both top_synsetid and top_synset_sense_count` — eliminating the duplicate inner subquery.
3. `top_synset` CTE — finds the best synset per word by querying the already-materialized synset_counts, so it's operating on a small in-memory table rather than the raw 212K-row senses table.

Result: **~2.7s vs timeout (>120s)** for the original.
