## Implementation Summary

### `ColumnParallelLinear`
• Weight sharding: Splits master_weight along dim=0 (output dimension) — each rank gets rows `[rank * (out/W) : (rank+1) * (out/W)], yielding a local weight of shape [out_features//world_size, in_features]`.
• Bias sharding: Each rank has a zero-initialized bias of shape `[out_features//world_size]`, matching its output shard.
• Forward: Computes local_out = x @ W_rank.T + b_rank, then performs an all_gather across ranks and concatenates along the last dimension to reconstruct the full [*, out_features] output.
• Gradient trick: The local_out tensor (which has a grad_fn) is re-inserted at the local rank's position in gather_list before torch.cat, so gradients flow correctly back through the local computation.

### `RowParallelLinear`
• Weight sharding: Splits master_weight along dim=1 (input dimension) — each rank gets columns `[rank * (in/W) : (rank+1) * (in/W)], yielding a local weight of shape [out_features, in_features//world_size]`.
• Bias: Full (un-sharded), zero-initialized, replicated on every rank — added only once after the reduction.
• Forward: Computes partial_out = x_local @ W_rank.T (no bias), then performs an **all_reduce SUM** to sum partial outputs from all ranks, then adds bias.
• Gradient correctness: Uses _AllReduceSum — a custom torch.autograd.Function whose backward pass also passes the gradient straight through (all_reduce SUM is self-adjoint).
