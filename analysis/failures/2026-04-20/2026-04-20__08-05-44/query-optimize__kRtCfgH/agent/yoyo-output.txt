The solution is saved at /app/sol.sql. Here's what I did and why it's much faster:

## Problem with the original query

The original query had two correlated subqueries in the SELECT clause that ran once per output row (500 times), and the second one contained a nested correlated subquery — making it O(n²) or worse against a 212K-row senses table. That's why it timed out.

## Optimizations applied

1. Eliminated all correlated subqueries — replaced them with CTEs computed once upfront:

   • `word_synset_counts` CTE: pre-aggregates `COUNT(*) per (wordid, synsetid)` pair in a single scan of senses.
   • `word_stats` CTE: uses those pre-computed counts to derive all per-word stats (total_senses, total_synsets, distinct_domains, distinct_posids) with the HAVING filter applied.
   • `top_synsets` CTE: uses `ROW_NUMBER() OVER (PARTITION BY wordid ORDER BY synset_sense_count DESC, synsetid ASC)` — a window function — to find the top synset per word in one pass, replacing the double-nested correlated subquery.

2. Computed `synset_sense_count` once and reused it in both word_stats and top_synsets, avoiding redundant scans.

Result: ~2.8 seconds vs. >2 minutes (original timed out), with identical output.
