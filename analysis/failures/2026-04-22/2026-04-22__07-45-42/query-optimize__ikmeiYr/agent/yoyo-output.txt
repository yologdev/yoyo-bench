The solution is ready. Here's what I did to optimize the query:

Problems with the original:
1. **Two deeply-nested correlated subqueries** per output row — for each of the 500 rows, SQLite executed a subquery to find top_synsetid, then *another* correlated subquery (with a nested correlated subquery inside) to find top_synset_sense_count. With ~152K words, this was catastrophically slow (~90 seconds just for 10 rows).

Optimizations applied:
1. **sense_agg CTE* — Pre-aggregates `COUNT() per (wordid, synsetid)` once, instead of recomputing it per row.
2. **word_synset_stats CTE with window functions** — Uses SUM(...) OVER, COUNT(...) OVER, and RANK() OVER to compute total_senses, total_synsets, and the ranking for top_synsetid in a single pass, eliminating all correlated subqueries.
3. `word_domain_pos` CTE — Pre-aggregates `distinct_domains and distinct_posids` once for all words.
4. `filtered` CTE — Filters by the HAVING conditions early (before joining with words and domain/pos data), reducing the data that needs to be joined.

The result: ~3 seconds vs >90 seconds (and likely >10 minutes for the full 500 rows) for the original.
