## Design Summary

### Assembly Strategy
The output plasmid is assembled from 4 fragments using one-pot Golden Gate Assembly with BsaI-HFv2. The input plasmid backbone is PCR-amplified (spanning the region that excludes the original gene), and the three insert sequences (egfp, flag, snap) are amplified with their ATG/stop codons adjusted to create a continuous fusion protein:
• EGFP: amplified without its stop codon
• FLAG: amplified without its start ATG and without its stop codon (fused in-frame)
• SNAP: amplified without its start ATG (fused in-frame, keeps its stop codon)

### BsaI Golden Gate Primer Structure
Each primer follows: `5'-GCGC-GGTCTC-A-[overhang/annealing]-3'`
• GCGC = extra bases for efficient BsaI cutting near DNA ends
• `GGTCTC` = BsaI recognition sequence
• A = 1-nt spacer (BsaI cuts 1 nt downstream on top strand, exposing 4-nt 5' overhang)

### 4-nt Overhangs (all unique, non-palindromic)
| Junction | Overhang |
|----------|----------|
| input → EGFP | ATGA |
| EGFP → FLAG | GGTA |
| FLAG → SNAP | GACA |
| SNAP → input | TGAG |
